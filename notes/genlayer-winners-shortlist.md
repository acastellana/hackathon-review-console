# GenLayer winners shortlist

Principle used:
- Bias toward **core GenLayer usage** over generic product polish
- Prefer projects where GenLayer is doing real work: consensus, intelligent-contract execution, validation, settlement, or judgment
- Use confidence/evidence to break ties

## Winner tier / strongest GenLayer-native candidates

### 1. TruthMarket
- Why: one of the clearest GenLayer-native uses in the field
- GenLayer role: AI validator consensus resolves prediction markets
- Why it stands out: strong conceptual fit + explicit README/transcript evidence

### 2. Hyphe
- Why: explicitly framed as AI-native prediction markets on GenLayer intelligent contracts
- GenLayer role: core market execution / resolution layer
- Why it stands out: direct GenLayer identity, not incidental usage

### 3. Rebyt
- Why: payout and release logic depend on GenLayer validator consensus
- GenLayer role: settlement / outcome verification
- Why it stands out: concrete use of validator consensus on a real transactional flow

### 4. AutoBounty
- Why: uses GenLayer validators to verify GitHub PRs/issues for bounty settlement
- GenLayer role: decentralized AI verification layer
- Why it stands out: very crisp product-to-GenLayer mapping

### 5. VeriTask
- Why: freelance escrow with AI-verified work / dispute logic built on GenLayer
- GenLayer role: escrow verification + arbitration
- Why it stands out: strong GenLayer use on a classic high-value escrow problem

## Finalist tier
- **Proven** — strong GenLayer settlement story for challenge markets
- **BuildersClaw** — on-chain AI judging is a good GenLayer use, though broader stack is mixed
- **TrustWork** — very direct intelligent-contract escrow use case
- **TreasuryPilot** — credible governance/policy consensus use, but product complexity may dilute clarity
- **PetCare** — AI council validation on GenLayer is meaningful and easy to explain
